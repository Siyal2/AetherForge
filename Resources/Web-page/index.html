Strawberry üçì

The **strawberry** is a widely grown hybrid species of the genus *Fragaria*, collectively known as the strawberries. It is cultivated worldwide for its fruit. The fruit is widely appreciated for its characteristic aroma, bright red color, juicy texture, and sweetness.

Did you know strawberries are not true berries? They are aggregate accessory fruits!

Strawberry Nutrition

  • Rich in Vitamin C
  • Good source of Manganese
  • High in Antioxidants

Strawberries are delicious in salads, desserts, and smoothies. [Image of a bowl of fresh strawberries]

Blueberry ü´ê

**Blueberries** are perennial flowering plants with indigo-colored berries from the section *Cyanococcus* within the genus *Vaccinium*. Species in the section *Cyanococcus* are the most common commercial blueberries. They are usually prostrate shrubs that can vary in size from 10 centimeters to 4 meters tall.

Blueberries are known for their high antioxidant content, especially anthocyanins, which give them their blue color.

Blueberry Benefits

  • Boosts brain health
  • Supports heart health
  • Aids digestion

Enjoy blueberries in pancakes, muffins, or simply as a snack. [Image of a handful of blueberries]

Raspberry üî¥

**Raspberries** are the edible fruit of a multitude of plant species in the genus *Rubus* of the rose family. The name also applies to these plants themselves. Raspberries are perennial with woody stems.

There are many varieties of raspberries, including red, black, purple, and golden raspberries.

Raspberry Varieties

  • Red Raspberries
  • Black Raspberries
  • Golden Raspberries

Raspberries are excellent in pies, jams, and tarts. [Image of fresh raspberries on a vine]

Blackberry ‚ö´

**Blackberries** are an edible fruit produced by many species in the genus *Rubus* in the family Rosaceae, hybrids among these species within the subgenus *Rubus*, and hybrids between the subgenus *Rubus* and species in other *Rubus* subgenera. They are aggregate fruits, composed of multiple small drupelets.

Blackberries are often confused with black raspberries, but they are distinct! Blackberries have a solid core, while black raspberries are hollow.

Blackberry Facts

  • Rich in fiber
  • Good for skin health
  • Versatile in cooking

Blackberries are great in crumbles, cobblers, and as a topping for yogurt. [Image of ripe blackberries]

Cranberry üü•

**Cranberries** are a group of evergreen dwarf shrubs or trailing vines in the subgenus *Oxycoccus* of the genus *Vaccinium*. They are often found in acidic bogs in the cooler regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Cranberries are known for their tart taste and vibrant red color.

Cranberries are a traditional part of Thanksgiving meals in North America.

Cranberry Uses

  • Juices and sauces
  • Dried cranberries
  • Urinary tract health

Cranberries are popular in sauces, juices, and dried snacks. [Image of cranberries floating in water]
